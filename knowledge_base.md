# ORCare Clinical Knowledge Base

This document contains all the clinical knowledge, educational content, and preventive care guidelines used in the ORCare application.

## 1. Oral Diseases & Conditions

| Disease | Description | What People Notice | Why it Happens | Why not Ignore | When to See Dentist |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gingivitis** | Earliest reversible stage of gum disease. Plaque accumulation irritates gum tissue. | Red, puffy gums; bleeding during brushing/flossing; persistent bad breath. | Poor hygiene; hormonal changes; medications; smoking; diabetes. | Can progress to periodontitis, leading to bone loss and tooth loss. | Bleeding during cleaning; visible gum recession. |
| **Cavities (Tooth Decay)** | Demineralization of enamel by acids, eventually creating a hole. | White spots; sharp pain with sweet/hot/cold; visible pits; staining. | Mouth bacteria feed on sugars to create acid; frequent snacking; low fluoride. | Decay never stops on its own; can reach the nerve (pulp), causing abscesses. | Lingering toothache; sharp sensitivity; visible hole or rough edge. |
| **Bad Breath (Halitosis)** | Breakdown of proteins by anaerobic bacteria producing sulfur compounds. | Unpleasant taste; white/yellow coating on tongue; dry mouth. | Poor hygiene; dry mouth; sinus infections; gastric reflux; systemic issues. | Major warning sign of active gum disease or hidden infection. | Persistent odor after 2 weeks of improved hygiene. |
| **Oral Cancer** | Abnormal cell growth in mouth/throat tissues. | Sore/ulcer not healing in 14 days; red or white patches; lumps; difficulty swallowing. | Tobacco use (inc. gutka); excessive alcohol; prolonged sun; HPV infection. | Highly treatable (80-90% survival) if caught early; late detection reduces recovery. | Any sore, lump, or patch persisting for more than 2 weeks. |
| **Tooth Sensitivity** | Exposed dentin tubules lead directly to the tooth's nerve center. | Sharp flash of pain with cold air/water; hot coffee; acidic/sweet food. | Aggressive brushing; gum recession; teeth grinding; acidic erosion. | May mask cracked teeth or deep cavities; leads to avoiding cleaning. | Severe/localized pain; sensitive toothpaste doesn't help after 1 month. |
| **Mouth Ulcers (Canker Sores)** | Small, shallow lesions on soft tissues or base of gums. | Oval sores with white/yellow center and red border; tingling before appearance. | Injuries; stress; hormonal shifts; nutrient deficiencies (B12, Zinc, Iron). | Frequent outbreaks signal stress or nutrient deficiency; link to IBD. | Unusually large sores; spreading sores; fever or difficulty drinking. |

## 2. Learning Center Content

### Category 1: Daily Oral Hygiene
*   **Module: Daily Practices (5 min)**
    *   *Lesson 1*: Oral hygiene is about removing microbial biofilm (plaque) to prevent 90% of dental problems.
    *   *Lesson 2*: Four pillars: Brush effectively 2x/day, floss 1x/day, scrape tongue, stay hydrated.
    *   *Lesson 3*: Saliva neutralizes acids and remineralizes enamel; water supports production.
    *   *Lesson 4*: Night-time brushing is most critical as saliva production drops.
    *   *Lesson 5*: Gentle precision is better than force; scrubbing causes gum recession.
    *   *Lesson 6*: Rinse with water after meals if you cannot brush.
*   **Module: Mastering Brushing (6 min)**
    *   Technique: 45-degree angle at gum line (Bass technique), vibratory circles, vertical flick for back of front teeth.
    *   Rule: Two-minute rule, 30 seconds per quadrant.
*   **Module: Flossing Guide (5 min)**
    *   Fact: Brushing misses 35% of tooth surfaces (the sides).
    *   Technique: C-shape mastery, avoiding the "snap", using clean sections for each tooth.
*   **Module: Tongue Care (3 min)**
    *   Fact: Back of the tongue is the primary source of halitosis; use a scraper or brush from back to front.

### Category 2: Prevention & Tools
*   **Module: Your Dental Toolkit**
    *   Soft/Extra-soft bristles; Fluoride toothpaste (pea-sized); Change brush Every 3 months; Tongue scraper; Store brush upright in open air.
*   **Module: Mouthwash Basics**
    *   Not a substitute for brushing; choose alcohol-free; don't rinse immediately after brushing (keep fluoride).
*   **Module: Nutrition for Health**
    *   Calcium & Vitamin D (Strengthen); Vitamin C (Gums); Crunchy veggies (Clean teeth); Water (Fluoride & Wash).

## 3. Daily Tips (Hygiene, Food, Lifestyle)

1.  **Brush at Night**: The most important brushing session to prevent 7-8 hours of bacterial acid production.
2.  **2 Minutes Rule**: Minimum time needed for effective cleaning.
3.  **Tongue Cleaning**: Essential for fresh breath and removing bacteria.
4.  **Replace Brush**: Every 3 months or after illness.
5.  **Spit, Don't Rinse**: Keep fluoride on teeth after brushing.
6.  **Sugar Control**: Frequency of sugar is more harmful than quantity (Limit acid attacks).
7.  **Tobacco Warning**: High risk of gum disease and oral cancer.
8.  **Hydration**: Saliva is your natural protector.
